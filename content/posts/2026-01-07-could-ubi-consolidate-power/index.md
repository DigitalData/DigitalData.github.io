Universal basic income (UBI) originally entered my pool of knowledge when Andrew Yang ran as a democratic candidate in 2020 for the USA presidential election. 
It was his key talking point, and at the time I thought it was an interesting idea (bearing in mind that I was a high-schooler who had not engaged with economics up to that point). 
The premise, as I understand it, is simple: In order to support people displaced from work by automation, everyone would receive a standard income from the government. 
Applying an individual's lens, this could theoretically help with costs of living. H
owever, my concern nowadays is about those of us who are technically not in need of this proposed income and will likely not be displaced by automation in the future.

I fear that, as many are displaced from earning supplemental income to the universal basic income, those who continue to earn will be at a significant relative advantage while the economic continues to be transactional and monetary.
